# Clarify Intents

Clarify Intents gather information before action when evidence, niche legibility, source-of-truth mapping or boundary conditions are unclear.

In the adaptive organism model, Clarify Intents are sensory and semantic moves. They improve perception before the organism acts.

## When To Clarify

Create a Clarify Intent when:

- the external niche is too opaque
- the source-of-truth outcome is undefined
- the claim boundary is unclear
- the risk cannot be bounded
- the expected feedback would be noisy
- the operator is overloaded
- the action would create hidden dependency
- the signal may be a bubble, trend wrapper or vanity metric

## Clarification Types

- **Sensor clarification**: gather a better signal.
- **Semantic clarification**: define the object, metric, claim or category.
- **Source-of-truth clarification**: identify the authority for the outcome.
- **Niche clarification**: learn whether the audience, partner, market or platform context is real enough.
- **Risk clarification**: identify legal, security, privacy, claim or continuity exposure.
- **Portability clarification**: test whether a pattern survives a wrapper, segment, timing or context change.

## Light Node Version

For a Light Node, a Clarify Intent can be a simple card:

```text
Before acting, I need to know [uncertainty].
The smallest useful check is [clarification step].
The result should change the decision by [decision relevance].
```

Clarify Intents prevent the system from converting vague signals into false action and false banking.
