# Action Intents

Action Intents are proposed interventions routed through Auto, Draft, Escalate or Blocked gates.

In the adaptive organism model, Action Intents are safe effectors: bounded ways for the business to change its environment, product, process, market wrapper, customer relationship or internal operation.

## Required Questions

Every Action Intent should answer:

- What business DNA or scaling hypothesis does this protect or test?
- Which adaptive field does it act in?
- What signal triggered it?
- What source-of-truth outcome will show whether it worked?
- What would count as useful learning?
- What could go wrong?
- Is the action reversible?
- What human approval gate applies?
- When should the delayed review happen?
- What script status might result?

## Gates

- **Auto**: safe, reversible, low-risk and already policy-approved.
- **Draft**: AI or workflow may prepare the action, but human review is required.
- **Escalate**: requires senior, legal, financial, clinical, privacy, claims or strategic judgement.
- **Blocked**: unsafe, unclear, non-compliant, not source-of-truth grounded or outside current capacity.

## Light Node Version

For a Light Node, an Action Intent can be a decision card:

```text
This week I will do [bounded action]
because I believe [hypothesis].
I will check [source-of-truth outcome]
on [delayed review date].
I will stop or revise if [risk / stop rule].
```

An Action Intent should never bypass judgement over claims, money, strategy, privacy or risk.
