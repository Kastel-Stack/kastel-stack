# State-Niche Fit

State-niche fit is Kastel's core coupling test:

```text
Is the business internally ready enough to act,
and is the external niche legible enough
for the action to generate useful learning?
```

In the adaptive organism model, state-niche fit asks whether the organism's current internal state is matched to its ecology well enough for a bounded action, clarification move or recovery step.

## Fit Inputs

Internal readiness:

- cash and delivery capacity
- operator workload
- source-of-truth availability
- claim and compliance safety
- security and recovery posture
- current open commitments
- tool and platform reliability
- human review capacity

External legibility:

- customer or partner signal clarity
- market / niche specificity
- platform context
- regulatory context
- timing
- proof requirements
- expected feedback delay
- competitive or category noise
- bubble / overload warning signs

Action interpretability:

- expected outcome
- source-of-truth outcome
- measurable delay
- possible confounds
- rollback or stop rule
- known failure modes
- portability check

## Fit States

Suggested labels:

- **In band**: ready enough to act and observe.
- **Clarify first**: niche or signal is too opaque for direct action.
- **Maintain first**: internal metabolism or delivery capacity needs attention before growth action.
- **Protect first**: immune-system issue is more important than adaptation.
- **Overloaded**: workload, risk or dependency burden makes action unreliable.
- **Locked-in**: too little exploration; increase entropy carefully.
- **Spun-out**: too much exploration; reduce entropy and constrain the loop.
- **Cascade-forming**: possible controlled scaling signal; increase review and quality constraints.

## Weekly Decision Rule

Action is preferred when:

```text
internal readiness is adequate
+ external niche is legible
+ source-of-truth outcome is defined
+ risk is bounded
+ workload is acceptable
+ delayed review is scheduled
```

Clarification is preferred when the action would produce noisy or uninterpretable evidence.

Maintenance is preferred when the organism's metabolism is weak.

Protection is preferred when the immune system has detected a serious exposure.

## Light Node Version

For a Light Node, state-niche fit can be reduced to four questions:

1. Are we stable enough to act this week?
2. Is the opportunity or risk clear enough to learn from?
3. Can we measure what happens?
4. Would this action create too much risk or workload?

If the answer is no, create a Clarify Intent, maintenance action or recovery action before pursuing growth.
